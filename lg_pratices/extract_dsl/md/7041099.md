## Resolution

- We would strongly recommendsetting the value of negative_dentry_limitto 10 (i.e. 1% of total RAM).


```
crash> p negative_dentry_limit
negative_dentry_limit = $10 = 0 <<------- 10
```

## Root Cause

- Lots of CPUs were getting busy with slab shrinking for page reclaiming. The dentry slab cache was quite largely bloated due to a huge number of negative dentries at that time.
- Soft lockups happened on lots of CPUs that were getting stuck waiting on dcache_lru_lock spinlock before carrying out slab shrinking for page reclaiming.
- This caused the rcu_sched CPU stalls detected on multiple CPUs in the subsequent APIC timer interrupt contexts.
- Since it's quite time consuming to write a huge amount of rcu_sched messages and call traces to the console and also it has to be done with the rnp->lock spinlock being held, the rnp->lock spinlock was severely contended and caused the CPU to invoke the hard lockup and the crash in the end.
