## Issue

- We previously resolveda similar deadlockby implementing an RHEL-only patch that modified SCHED_WARN_ON() to use WARN_ONCE_SAFE() instead of WARN_ONCE(). However, that is not applicable in this case.Here, the issue arises from WARN_ONCE(), not SCHED_WARN_ON(), being triggered when a PF_MEMALLOC task flushes a !WQ_MEM_RECLAIM workqueue in check_flush_dependency(). This behavior remains unchanged even in the latest upstream version.

## Root Cause

- FrameBuffer driver queues a work and also takes a lock in its console writing path, which can lead a deadlock just like this.


```

-----

   start_flush_work()
               spin_lock_irq(&pool->lock); <<----+
    check_flush_dependency()                     |
     __warn_printk()                             |
          ...                                    |
      vt_console_print()                         |
       hide_cursor()                             |
        bit_cursor()                             |
         soft_cursor()                       *DEADLOCK*   
          drm_fb_helper_sys_imageblit()          |
           drm_fb_helper_dirty()                 |
            schedule_work()                      |
             queue_work_on()                     |
              __queue_work()                     |
               spin_lock(&pwq->pool->lock) <<----+
```
