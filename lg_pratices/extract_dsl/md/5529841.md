## Root Cause

An occasional overflow can occur when calculating the time withingetnstimeofday64which causes calculating seconds and nanoseconds to take an extremely long period of time leading to a hard lockup.

`getnstimeofday64`During calculating the time of day, the kernel converts the clocksource delta between checks to nanoseconds. This conversion is done using signed math [2], but the delta is unsigned [1]. In case of a multiplication overflow, the conversion, therefore, becomes negative [2]. With binary, a negative number is denoted as negative by a leading 1 bit. Shifting a signed integer right preserves the sign [3] thus the value was padded on the left with leading 1's. As a result, calculating the seconds from elapsed nanoseconds can take an extremely long time [4] and lead to hard lockups.
