## Resolution

- We would recommend checking if the hypervisor was busy and quite highly loaded, running lots of VMs during the time frame when the issue occurred on this VM.
- Additionally, consider relocating this VM to a less busy hypervisor or migrating other VMs from the current hypervisor to alternative hosts to alleviate its workload and reduce overburden.


## Root Cause

- A hard lockup is defined as a bug that causes a cpu to loop in kernel mode with interrupts disabled for more than 10 seconds without giving other tasks a chance to run.
- In this case, the task is in spinlock busy-wait in get_partial_node() despite the list_lock spinlock not being locked, suggesting a vCPU lag.
- A vCPU lag occurs when a virtual CPU (vCPU) of a virtual machine (VM) doesn't get enough physical CPU time from the hypervisor, often due to high load on the hypervisor or over commitment of resources.
- This can cause the VM to experience delays or hang, as its vCPUs are waiting for the physical CPU time they need to process tasks.
- When the hypervisor is heavily loaded with many other VMs or other processes, the vCPUs may struggle to be scheduled efficiently, leading to performance degradation or VM hangs.

 - lru is the list head within each page structure.
