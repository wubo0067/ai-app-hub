## Resolution

resolution

- To prevent the issue, please avoid runningsysrq-tto write out a large amount of data into a serial console.
- ReferenceWhat is the SysRq Facility and how do I use it? - Red Hat Customer Portal
- We are aware of one possible mitigation that we do not use tasklist read lock in print_rq() (use rcu_read_lock() instead) so that a tasklist writer doesn't need to wait for all readers, one of which is taking tasklist read lock and writing out a large amount of sysrq-t data to serial console, to go away quite a long time.
- Our engineering team is now working on a private BZ#1797429 for that change.

To prevent the issue, please avoid runningsysrq-tto write out a large amount of data into a serial console.

## Root Cause

- A task took tasklist read lock in print_rq() and was writing out a call traces of all tasks/threads on the system into a serial console, which was very slow.
- At the same time, another one task was waiting for all tasklist readers to go away to set the locked flag with interrupts disabled.
- As a result hard LOCKUP occurred and the kernel crashed.

A task took tasklist read lock in print_rq() and was writing out a call traces of all tasks/threads on the system into a serial console, which was very slow.

As a result hard LOCKUP occurred and the kernel crashed.
