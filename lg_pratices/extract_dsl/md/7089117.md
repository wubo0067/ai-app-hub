## Resolution

- We recommend confirming with VMware support whether the hypervisor was under high load during the time this issue occurred on the VM. As a preventive measure, you might want to consider migrating the VM to a less busy hypervisor.


## Root Cause

- vCPUs of a VM are merely processes for the hypervisor. The hypervisor can reschedule processes as needed, allowing the scheduler to give other processes a chance to run on the CPU. When the hypervisor is busy and under heavy load, it naturally reschedules the vCPU processes more frequently, giving other processes CPU time. During this time, the vCPU processes are virtually paused, and when they regain CPU time, this gap appears as a time jump from the VM's perspective. This is a well-known cause of these seemingly groundless lockups.
