## Resolution


### Red Hat Enterprise Linux 6

- Upgrade to kernel-2.6.32-754.el6 from ErrataRHSA-2018:1854or later


### Workaround

- Require usage of fscacheDowngrade to a kernel earlier than 2.6.32-642.el6, for example, 2.6.32-573.8.1.el6
- Do not require usage of fscacheDisable fscache.  Follow the Resolution section ofFS-cache is automatically enabled in kernel-2.6.32-642.el6 or later for NFS mount whose mount option does not have 'fsc' option when cachefilesd is running.to ensure fscache is disabled.

## Root Cause

- Under investigation.
- Some condition leads to a seemingly indefinite non-zero value offscache_cookie.n_activewhich is the "number of active users of netfs ptrs".  This causes a soft lockup inside fscache module__fscache_disable_cookiefunction when an attempt is made to wait for this value to go to 0.  One of the following two possibilities seem likely:1) Either an over increment or fail to decrementfscache_cookie->n_active2) Some other active thread in the system / vmcore causesfscache_cookie->n_activeto never become 0.
- The following commit is missing from RHEL6 which would avoid the softlockup and allow other processes to run.  It is possible the following commit may resolve the soft lockup, or that other patches will be required.

`fscache_cookie.n_active``__fscache_disable_cookie``fscache_cookie->n_active``fscache_cookie->n_active`
